Travis Scott didn't just collaborate with Nike; he rewired the entire sneaker economy. By aggressively utilizing earth tones, backward Swooshes, and stash pockets, he turned the Houston rodeo aesthetic into global luxury.

His influence shifted the market away from high-tech basketball silhouettes back to retro models, single-handedly resurrecting the Nike SB Dunk in the late 2010s. When Jacques Webster wears a shoe, the secondary market reacts within minutes.
